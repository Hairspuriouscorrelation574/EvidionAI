The Problem

Research moves at the speed of a researcher.
Until now.

Scientific progress is bottlenecked not by lack of intelligence — but by the mechanics of research: the weeks spent in literature search, the months of iterative coding, the constant context-switching between tools, databases, and frameworks.

Evidion collapses that timeline. A researcher poses a question in natural language. The system takes it from there — autonomously planning, searching, computing, questioning its own conclusions, and delivering a structured report.

The result: hours instead of months. Human researchers stay where they belong — asking sharper questions, not running mechanical tasks.

How It Works

Five agents. One research goal.

Each agent is purpose-built for a distinct phase of scientific inquiry. Together, they form a closed-loop research system that plans, executes, and critically evaluates its own work before producing results.

Orchestration Layer
Supervisor Agent
The central intelligence that owns the research strategy. It decomposes the query into a research plan, dispatches tasks to specialist agents, evaluates their outputs, decides when to iterate versus conclude, and composes the final report.
Discovery
Search Agent
Retrieves and ranks evidence from scientific literature, preprint servers, and the web. Synthesises source material into structured context the other agents can reason over.
Computation
Code Agent
Writes and executes experiments in an isolated Python environment. Produces numerical results, statistical analyses, and visualisations that ground the research in reproducible evidence.
Interpretation
Analysis Agent
Interprets experimental outputs and literature signals through the lens of domain knowledge. Identifies patterns, draws conclusions, and surfaces implications the raw data alone wouldn't reveal.
Quality Gate
Skeptic Agent
Actively challenges every conclusion before it reaches the final report. Flags weak evidence, logical gaps, and unexamined assumptions — ensuring research integrity by design, not by accident.
